CRICKET

Last Update: 25/9/2012

1. GENERAL RULES

  • If the scheduled venue is changed, all bets will be considered void.
  • Should a game / event be abandoned, suspended or postponed and fail to resume after 12 hours from the official kick off / start time, the result as it stands will be deemed void and all bets will be cancelled, except for those on Markets that have been unconditionally determined. The company's decision to cancel all bets in such an event is final and is regardless of any official decision by the event referee or relevant governing authority.

2. TEST MATCHES & COUNTY CHAMPIONSHIP MATCHES

  • Bets will stand based on the official result provided at least one ball has been bowled. In the event of a tie, dead heat rules will apply and bets on the draw will be settled as losing bets.
  • If a match is abandoned due to external interference, bets will be considered void.
  • If a match, where the draw is not offered and the result ends in a draw, all bets placed on the match will be considered void.

3. LIMITED OVERS MATCHES

  • In one-day matches, if the match is cut short or abandoned due to external interference, all bets will be settled according to the official competition rules. If the final result is determined by a bowl out, a super-overs or the toss of coin, all bets will be considered void.
  • If the match ends with a draw and the official result or the competition rules do not determine a winner, all bets will be considered void.
  • Where the Duckworth Lewis Method (D/L method) is used, all bets placed on "Highest 10 over Total" and "Highest Opening Partnership" will be considered void, unless the market has been unconditionally determined before the D/L method was implemented.
  • Should a new toss take place on a scheduled reserve day for One Day International, all bets during the last 45 minutes to the start at the original scheduled first day match will be considered void. This rule applies to all markets, except those have been unconditionally determined, such as "Win The Toss"

4. BET TYPES

Below is a breakdown of the rules for the individual bet types we offer for Cricket.

    4.1. Money Line

    • Predict who will win the match. This market will contain the two teams as betting selections.

    4.2. Match Betting (1 x 2)

    • Predict who will win the match. This market will contain the two teams and the draw as betting selections.

    4.3. Handicap

    • Predict who will win the match / period with the indicated handicap applied.

    4.4. Over / Under (Runs)

    • Predict whether the total number of runs scored will be over or under the indicated total line.
    • If a game is abandoned, Over / Under bets will only be settled when the market has been unconditionally determined as any further potential runs have no affect on the market result. In all other scenarios, bets will be considered void.

    4.5. Series Betting

    • If the designated number of matches is not completed, bets will be considered void.
    • We reserve the right to void any specialty market (such as Series Correct Score) relating to the series as a whole, unless settlement of bets have already been determined.

    4.6. Top Batsman / Bowler (Series)

    • Predict who will be the top Batsman or Bowler in a specific series or competition.
    • Bets placed on players who are selected but do not bat or do not field will be settled as losing bets.
    • In the event of players taking the same number of wickets, the winner will be determined as the bowler that has conceded the least number of runs, irrespective of overs bowled by each player.
    • Dead-Heat rule applies if no winner has been determined.

    4.7. Top Batsman / Bowler (Match)

    • Predict who will be the top Batsman or Bowler in a specific match.
    • Bets placed on any player not in the starting 11 or nominated as the designated substitute 12th man will then be considered void.
    • In a 2-innings match (test match or county championship), this market applies to the first innings only, unless otherwise stated.
    • Bets placed on players who are selected but do not bat or do not field will be settled as losing bets.
    • In the event of players taking the same number of wickets, the winner will be determined as the bowler that has conceded the least number of runs, irrespective of overs bowled by each player.
    • Dead-Heat rule applies if no winner has been determined.

    4.8. Most Wickets

    • Bet on the player taking the most number of wickets during a match or specific innings.
    • Both players must bowl at least one ball for bets to be valid.

    4.9. Most Sixes

    • Bet on the team scoring the most number of sixes during a match or specific innings.
    • Bets will be considered valid if an official result is declared. If "No Result" is the official result then all bets on this market will be considered void.

    4.10. Next Man Out

    • Bet on the batsman that will be dismissed first in a match or specific innings.
    • If one batsman retires, all bets will be considered void. If neither batsman is dismissed then all bets will be considered void.
