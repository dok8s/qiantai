Select Sport Rules:

MOTOR SPORT

Last Update: 25/9/2012

1. GENERAL RULES

  • If the scheduled venue is changed, all bets will be void.
  • Podium positions will count as the official results, regardless of any subsequent disqualification or amendment to the result.
  • Bets will be settled based on the driver / rider ranking after the specified race according to the official FIA / FIM results.
  • If a race / qualifying is postponed and rescheduled to start within the following 12 hours of the original schedule start time, then all bets will stand. If the race is postponed for more than 12 hours, all bets will be considered void.
  • If a race / qualifying is suspended and scheduled to restart within the following 12 hours of the original schedule start time, then all bets will stand.

2. BET TYPES

Below is a breakdown of the rules for the individual bet types we offer for Motor Sport.

    2.1. Outright

    • If a driver / rider fails to start the race, all bets will still be considered valid.

    2.2. Head to Head

    • If one of the two drivers / riders does not start the race, all bets will be void
    • Bets will be settled based upon one driver's / rider's ranking against the other driver / rider after the specified race.
    • If both drivers / riders fail to finish a race, then bets will be void.
    • If both drivers / riders start a specific race and only one fails to finish, then the driver / rider who completes the race will be deemed the winner.

    2.3. Handicap

    • A Handicap market allows one driver / rider to get a virtual finishing position advantage over another driver / rider.
    • Both drivers / riders need to have a classified position based on the official FIA / FIM results in order for the bets to stand.
    • If either of the two drivers / riders are unable to complete the race, no matter what the reason, all bets will be considered void.
    • Bets will be settled based upon one driver's / rider's ranking against the other driver / rider after the specified race.
      • Here is an example of a Handicap market
        • League Time Home/Away Handicap
        F1 Italy GP 13/09
        8:00PM        		 Lewis Hamilton 1.5 0.93
        Fernando Alonso   0.93

        Race Results

        Lewis Hamilton 3rd Place
        Fernando Alonso 4th Place

        Bet Results

        Fernando Alonso moves ahead of Lewis Hamilton, in the Handicap market, based on the 1.5 place advantage he received at the start of the race.

    2.4. Fastest Lap

    • Predict which driver will record the fastest lap in the specific race.
    • Qualifying and practice laps do not count.

    2.5. Number of Classified Finishers

    • Predict which how many drivers / riders will finish the specific race.

    2.6. Winning Margin

    • Predict the time difference between the winning driver / rider and the runner-up at the end of the race.

    2.7. Top 3 Finishing Position (Podium Result)

    • Predict the first, second and third place drivers / riders in the specific race.
    • Podium positions are final and any change of result after the race will not count towards settlement.

    2.8. Odd / Even

    • Predict whether the driver's / rider's final position will be an odd or even number
    • If the driver / rider fails to complete the race, all bets will be void.
