Last Update: 05/21/2013

1. GENERAL RULES

  • Unless otherwise stated, all Football bets will be settled based on the scheduled "90 minutes" play.
  • The term "90 minutes" play includes any stoppage or injury time. This does not include the extra-time, golden goals, penalty shoot-outs, or a match result which was altered by the official referee or the relevant governing authority after the match.
  • All In Play bets will be settled based on the outcome at the end of the scheduled "90 minutes" play, unless otherwise stated in the individual rule for the bet type.
  • For certain competitions or friendly matches where the scheduled play is 80 Minutes (2 x 40 minute halves), bets will be settled based on the scheduled time.
  • Should there be any youth or friendly matches where play is set for 70 minutes (2 x 35 minute halves) or less, the company will announce before the start of the match. Otherwise all bets on these matches will be considered void.
  • If the match is abandoned at anytime within the official 90 minutes of play, all bets will be considered void, unless explicitly stated below or in the individual Bet Type rules.
  • If a match is abandoned during the 1st half, all 1st half bets are considered void. If a match is abandoned during the 2nd half, all 2nd half bets are considered void, unless otherwise stated in the individual bet type rules. All 1st half bets will still be valid.
  • Own goals are counted for settlement purpose, unless otherwise stated in the individual bet type rules.
  • If the scheduled venue is changed, all bets will be considered void if the original away team is now the home team.
  • For International matches, as long as the change in venue is within same country, all bets are considered valid.
  • For International competitions, as long as the venue is within the country or countries where the competition was originally scheduled to be held, all bets are considered valid.
  • We reserve the right to void all bets if we think other change of venues scenarios could affect the outcome of the event.

    2.1. Handicap (Asian Handicap)

    Home/Away Handicap
    Arsenal(H) 0.5/1 0.97
    Manchester United   0.98

      2.1.1. General Rules

      • Predict who will win the match / half / period with the indicated handicap applied.
      • A "Handicap" means that a team receives a virtual head start, effectively leading the match by differing goals before it actually begins.
      • All bets will be settled by factoring in the indicated handicap applied at the end of the bet type period.
      • The favourite team, giving the handicap start, will be allocated the minus handicap and will be highlighted.
      • A handicap can be a full goal start (e.g. -1, -2, -3, etc.), a half goal start (e.g. -0.5, -1.5, -2.5, etc.) or a split goal start (e.g. -0/0.5, -0.5/1, -1/1.5, etc.).

      2.1.2. Half Time Handicaps (Asian Handicap)

      • All 1st half bets apply to the first half of play only. Bets are settled on the score at the end of the scheduled "45 minutes" including any injury time.
      • If the match is abandoned, suspended, cancelled or aborted for whatsoever reason during the 1st Half, all the first half bets will be considered void.
      • If the match is abandoned, suspended, cancelled or aborted for whatsoever reason during the 2nd Half or additional periods, all the first half bets will be considered valid.

      2.1.3. In-Play Handicap (Also called Running Ball)

      • All bets will be settled by factoring in the indicated handicap applied at the end of the bet type period.
      • Settlement is based on the score line, from when the bet was placed to the end of the match / period - i.e. the final score minus the current score of the match. For 1st Half Handicap bets, this is the final score at the end of the 1st Half.

    2.2. Over / Under Goals

      2.2.1. General Rules

      • Predict whether the total number of goals will be over or under the indicated goal line.
      • If the total number of goals scored is more than the indicated line, the market is settled as 'Over'. If the total number of goals scored is less than the indicated line, the market is settled as 'Under'.
      • All Over / Under bets will be settled by factoring in the indicated handicap applied at the end of the bet type period.
      • An Over / Under market line can be a full goal (e.g. 2, 3, 4, etc.), a half goal (e.g. 1.5, 2.5, 3.5, etc.) or a split goal line (e.g. 1.5/2, 2.5/3, 3.5/4, etc.).
      • If a match is abandoned, Over / Under bets will only be settled when the market has been unconditionally determined and any further goals have no affect on the market result. In all other scenarios, bets will be considered void. Please see the examples below:
        • Example 1: Member bets Over 2.5 Goals:
          • The match is abandoned when the score is 2 - 1
          • The member wins as, even though the match is abandoned, this market has been unconditionally determined and any other potential goals have no affect on the market result.
        • Example 2: Member bets Under 2.5 Goals:
          • The match is abandoned when the score is 2 - 1
          • The member's bet is a losing bet as, even though the match is abandoned, this market has been unconditionally determined and any other potential goals have no affect on the market result.
        • Example 3: Member bets Over 3.5 Goals:
          • The match is abandoned when the score is 2 - 1
          • The member's bet is considered void as the match was abandoned before the result of the market is unconditionally determined.

      2.2.2. Half Time Over / Under Goals

      • All 1st half bets apply to the first half of play only. Bets are settled on the score at the end of the scheduled "45 minutes" including any injury time.
      • If the match is abandoned, suspended, cancelled or aborted for whatsoever reason during the 1st Half, all the first half bets will be considered void, unless the market has been unconditionally determined.
      • If the match is abandoned, suspended, cancelled or aborted for whatsoever reason during the 2nd Half or additional periods, all the first half bets will be considered valid.

      2.2.3. In-Play Over / Under Goals

      • Settlement is based on the final score and the goal line at time of bet placement is applied to a 0-0 score line, not the current score line. The current score, at the time of bet placement, is not factored into the bet.

      2.2.4. Single Team Over / Under Goals

      • Predict whether the total number of goals, scored by the named team, will be over or under the indicated goal line.
      • If the total number of goals scored is more than the indicated line, the market is settled as 'Over'. If the total number of goals scored is less than the indicated line, the market is settled as 'Under'.
      • If a match is abandoned, Single Team Over / Under bets will only be settled when the market has been unconditionally determined and any further goals have no affect on the market result. In all other scenarios, bets will be considered void.

      • Own Goal: The goal must be awarded as an own goal.
      • Header: The goal scorer must clearly use their head to score the goal.
      • Shot: All other methods of goal. All other goal types which are not included in the above methods are included here.

    Below is a breakdown of the rules for the individual Corner bet types we offer.

      2.34.1. Corners: General Rules

      • For settlement purposes, corners awarded but not taken will not count.

      2.34.4. First Corner / Last Corner

      • Predict the team to take the First or Last corner in a designated match within the official "90 minutes" play.
      • If a match is abandoned after the "First Corner" is taken, all bets on "First Corner" will be considered valid.

      2.34.5. Half with Most Corners

      • Predict which half will have the most corners taken after the full 90 minutes have been played, excluding extra time or penalty shoot-outs.
      • If a match is abandoned, any Half with Most Corners bets will only be settled when the result is unconditionally determined and any further corners have no affect on the market result. In all other scenarios, bets will be considered void.

      2.34.6. Time of the First Corner

      • Predict the time in which the first corner will be taken in the specific match.
      • An example of the selections are:

    Bookings / Cards

    2.35. Bookings / Cards

    Below is a breakdown of the rules for the individual Bookings / Cards bet types we offer.

      2.35.1. Bookings: General Rules

      • For settlement purposes, cards issued to non-players (e.g. managers or substitutes who play no subsequent part in the match) do not count.

    2.61. Total Home and Away Team in a Particular League

    In certain leagues, the company will offer the member the opportunity to bet across events to determine the outcome of the events combined. This market combines all the home team results against all the away team results. For neutral matches, Team 1 will be considered the 'Home Team' for this market. Below is a breakdown of the rules for the individual bet types we offer.

      2.61.1. Total Home and Away in a Particular League: General Rules

      • If one match in the particular league is abandoned or cancelled, Over / Under bets will only be settled when the market has been unconditionally determined and any further goals have no affect on the market result. In all other scenarios, bets will be considered void.
      • The day that the matches take place and the number of matches will be clearly listed in the selection name, for example:
        • Home Team - Friday - 3 Games
        • Away Team - Friday - 3 Games

      2.61.2. Total Home and Away in a Particular League: Handicap (Asian Handicap)

      • Predict the result of all the home teams against all the away teams with the indicated handicap applied through the full "90 minutes" of play, excluding extra time or penalty shoot-outs.

      2.61.3. Total Home and Away in a Particular League: Over / Under (Total)

      • Predict whether the total number of goals for both selections will be over or under the indicated line.
