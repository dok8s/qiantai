BASEBALL

Last Update: 25/9/2012

1. GENERAL RULES

  • If the scheduled venue is changed, all bets will be considered void.
  • International Baseball Rules: Games will end early if a team is leading by 10 or more runs after the opposing team has batted in at least 7 innings, or if a team is leading by 15 or more runs after the opposing team has batted in at least 5 innings. If this happens, all bets will be considered valid, including Money Line, Odd / Even, Run Line and Total Runs (Over/Under).
  • When betting on the Run Line and Total Runs (over/under) the game must conduct 9 innings (or 8.5 innings, when the 2nd batting team is already ahead) to have a result. If a game is called or suspended in an extra innings, the score will be determined after the last full inning unless the home team scores to tie, or takes the lead in the bottom half of the inning, in which case the score is determined at the time the game is called.
  • Should a game / event be abandoned, suspended or postponed and fail to resume after 12 hours from the official kick off / start time, the result as it stands will be deemed void and all bets will be cancelled, except for those on Markets that have been unconditionally determined. The company's decision to cancel all bets in such an event is final and is regardless of any official decision by the event referee or relevant governing authority.
  • Extra innings count for settlement purposes, unless otherwise specified.
  • 1st half betting is based on the result from the 1st to the end of the 5th period.
  • 2nd half betting is based on the result from the 6th period to the end of the 9th period. Extra innings count for settlement purposes on 2nd half betting.
  • All Bets placed will remain valid regardless of the starting pitcher.
  • If the game is suspended or cancelled during the 2nd half, all 1st half bets will still be valid.
  • If the game is suspended or cancelled during the 2nd half, all 2nd half bets will be considered void, unless otherwise stated in the individual bet type rules.
  • If the game starts before the scheduled time, only bets placed before the game commenced will be considered valid. Bets placed after the game commenced will be considered void. This excludes In-Play bet types.

2. BET TYPES

Below is a breakdown of the rules for the individual bet types we offer for Baseball.

    2.1. Money Line

    • Predict who will win the game.
    • Extra innings count for settlement purposes.

    2.2. Run Line (Handicap)

    • Predict who will win the game / period with the indicated handicap applied.
    • Extra innings count for settlement purposes.

    2.3. In-Play Run Line (Handicap)

    • Predict who will win the game / period with the indicated handicap applied.
    • Settlement is based on the score line, from when the bet was placed to the end of the game / period.
    • Extra innings count for settlement purposes.

    2.4. Total Runs (Over / Under)

    • Predict whether the total number of runs scored will be over or under the indicated total line.
    • Extra innings count for settlement purposes.
    • If a game is abandoned, Over / Under bets will only be settled when the market has been unconditionally determined as any further potential runs have no affect on the market result. In all other scenarios, bets will be considered void.
    • If the game is abandoned during the 1st half, all 1st half bets will be void unless they have been unconditionally determined as any further potential runs have no affect on the market result.
    • If the game is abandoned during the 2nd half, all 1st half bets will still be valid.
    • If the game is abandoned during the 2nd half, all 2nd half bets will be void unless they have been unconditionally determined as any further potential runs have no affect on the market result.

    2.5. In-Play Total Runs (Over / Under)

    • Predict whether the total number of goals scored will be over or under the indicated total line.
    • Settlement is based on the final score line and the total line is applied to a 0-0 score line. The current score, at the time of bet placement, is not factored into the bet.
    • Extra innings count for settlement purposes.

    2.6. Odd / Even

    • Predict whether the total number of runs scored will be odd or even.
    • Extra innings count for settlement purposes.
